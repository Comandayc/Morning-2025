KAKUMA REFUGEE CAMP SINCE 1992

KAKUMA REFUGEE CAMP WAS ESTABLISHED IN 1992 WITH THE DIsPLACED COMMUNITY FROM SOUTH SUDAN DUE TO HEAVY WAR IN THE SOUTHERN PART OF THE COUNTRY, LOKI WAS THE MAIN RESCEPTION AREA OF THE ARRIVAL BEFORE ESTABLISHMENT OF THE KAKUMA REFUGEE CAMP IN THE YEAR 1992. TODAY IS MADE UP OF DIFFERENT NATIONALITIES THAT FLEE FROM THEIR COUNTRY DUE TO SEVERAL REASONS. HERE ARE THE FOLLLOWING NATIONALITIES IN THE CAMP. SOUTH SUDANESE, SUDANESE, NIGERIANS, UGANDAN,CONGOLESE,ETHIOPIAN,RWANDAN,BURUDIAN AND SOMALIAN.
